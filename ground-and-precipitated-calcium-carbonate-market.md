<p><p>Ground Calcium Carbonate (GCC) is produced by grinding natural calcium carbonate into a fine powder. It is commonly used in the paper, plastics, and construction industries due to its low cost and wide availability. Precipitated Calcium Carbonate (PCC), on the other hand, is produced by chemical precipitation. It has a more uniform particle size and higher brightness compared to GCC, making it suitable for applications in paints, coatings, and adhesives where higher quality and purity are required.</p></p>
