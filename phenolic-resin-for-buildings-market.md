<p><p>Phenolic resin is commonly used in building materials due to its heat resistance, durability, and fire-retardant properties. There are two main types of phenolic resin used in the market: thermoplastic and thermosetting. Thermoplastic phenolic resin can be heated and reformed multiple times, making it suitable for applications where flexibility is required. Thermosetting phenolic resin undergoes a chemical reaction during curing, creating a rigid and durable material suitable for structural components and high-temperature applications in buildings.</p></p>
