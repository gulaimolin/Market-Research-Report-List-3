<p><p>Biological glues can be categorized into different types based on their composition and properties. Proteins, such as fibrin and collagen, are commonly used as biological glues due to their strong adhesive qualities. Carbohydrates, like chitosan, offer biocompatibility and biodegradability. Glycoproteins, such as gelatin and elastin, provide flexibility and elasticity to the glue. Mucopolysaccharides, such as hyaluronic acid, offer viscosity and lubrication. Other types of biological glues may include synthetic polymers or bioactive compounds with unique adhesive properties.</p></p>
