<p><p>Time-of-flight cameras for smartphones can be divided into two main types: CMOS and CCD. CMOS time-of-flight cameras are more common in smartphones due to their lower cost and power consumption. They use a CMOS sensor to measure the time it takes for light to travel to and from objects in the scene, creating 3D depth maps. On the other hand, CCD time-of-flight cameras have better image quality and low noise performance, but they are less commonly used in smartphones due to their higher cost and power consumption.</p></p>
