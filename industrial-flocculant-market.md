<p><p>Industrial flocculants are used to help separate suspended particles from liquids in various industrial processes. Organic coagulants are derived from natural substances like starch or proteins, while inorganic coagulants are made from chemicals like aluminum sulfate or ferric chloride. Organic coagulants are generally more environmentally-friendly and have lower toxicity levels, but inorganic coagulants are often more effective in treating larger quantities of water. Both types of flocculants play important roles in water treatment, wastewater treatment, mining, and other industrial applications.</p></p>
