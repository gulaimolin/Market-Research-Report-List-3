<p><p>Contact displacement sensors are commonly categorized into two types - analog and digital. Analog sensors provide continuous output signals that vary based on the amount of displacement, allowing for precise measurements. On the other hand, digital sensors provide discrete output signals in the form of binary data, making them easier to interface with digital systems. Both types of sensors are widely used in various industries such as automotive, manufacturing, and robotics for monitoring and controlling processes that require accurate displacement measurements.</p></p>
