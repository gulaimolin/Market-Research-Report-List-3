<p><p>Dry lubricants are solid lubricating materials that reduce friction between surfaces without the use of oils or liquids. The types of dry lubricants include Graphite, Molybdenum Disulfide, Boron Nitride, PTFE (polytetrafluoroethylene), Soft-metal Solid Lubricants, and Others. Graphite and Molybdenum Disulfide are commonly used for high friction applications, while Boron Nitride and PTFE are used for high temperature applications. Soft-metal solid lubricants offer low friction and good wear resistance. Other types of dry lubricants include ceramic-based and polymer-based materials.</p></p>
