<p><p>Defatted soya flour is categorized into two main market types based on the level of denaturation: low denatured and high denatured. Low denatured defatted soya flour undergoes minimal processing, preserving more of the natural nutrients and proteins. It is often used in food products where higher protein content is desired. On the other hand, high denatured defatted soya flour is more processed, resulting in a lower protein content but also a smoother texture and better binding properties, making it suitable for use in applications such as baked goods and meat products.</p></p>
