<p><p>Carbon fiber composites are classified into three main types based on the raw materials used in their production. Pitch-based carbon fibers are derived from coal or petroleum pitch, offering high strength and stiffness. Polyacrylonitrile (PAN) carbon fibers are produced from acrylic polymers and boast exceptional mechanical properties. Rayon-based carbon fibers are made from cellulose fibers and are known for their excellent performance in high-temperature environments. Each type caters to different industries and applications, contributing to the diversification of the carbon fiber composite market.</p></p>
