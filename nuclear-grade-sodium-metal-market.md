<p><p>Nuclear grade sodium metal is classified into two types based on its physical state: liquid type and solid type. Liquid type sodium metal is in a molten state at high temperatures and is commonly used in liquid metal fast breeder reactors. On the other hand, solid type sodium metal is used in solid sodium-cooled fast reactors. Both types of sodium metal play crucial roles in nuclear power generation, with liquid type sodium being more commonly used due to its higher heat transfer efficiency.</p></p>
