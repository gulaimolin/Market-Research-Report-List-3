<p><p>Flat heat pipes come in two main market types: Ultra Thin and Standard. Ultra Thin flat heat pipes are designed for applications where thinness and lightweight are critical, such as in aerospace and electronics. They are typically less than 0.5 mm thick and provide efficient heat transfer capabilities. Standard flat heat pipes, on the other hand, are more commonly used in general thermal management applications across various industries. They are typically thicker and offer high thermal conductivity for effective heat dissipation.</p></p>
